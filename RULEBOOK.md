# 🚀 FUTURE SPRINT 2026 Rulebook

**8 Hours. One Idea. Built for Impact.**

FUTURESPRINT 2026 is an **open-theme hackathon** providing a focused platform for students to transform ideas into working prototypes within a constrained time frame. The event is organized by the **School of Engineering** and the **School of Info. Sc. & Tech.** at **St Aloysius (Deemed to be University), Mangaluru**.

### 📋 Event Overview
*   **Eligibility:** Open to **Students of St Aloysius (Deemed to be University)**.
*   **Theme:** **Open Theme**.
*   **Venue:** **AIMIT CAMPUS**.
*   **Cash Prizes:** **₹5K, ₹3K & ₹2K**.
*   **Perks:** **All Registered Teams Receive Certificates**.

---

## 🟢 Phase 1: Registration & Shortlisting

**👥 Team Formation**
*   Each team must consist of **3-4 members**.
*   **Team composition cannot be changed** after registration.

**📝 Registration**
*   All teams must register **on or before March 23, 2026**.
*   Registration fee: **₹50 per participant**.
*   Registration Link: **[https://futuresprint.soefest.com/](https://futuresprint.soefest.com/)**

**📄 Abstract Submission**
*   Teams must submit a brief abstract outlining the:
    1.  **Problem Statement**.
    2.  **Proposed Solution**.
    3.  **Approach/Technology** to be used.

**✅ Shortlisting**
*   Based on the abstracts, the **Top 25 teams** will be shortlisted.
*   Results will be announced on **March 24, 2026**.

---

## 🔵 Phase 2: Hackathon Finale

**📅 Event Details**
*   Date: **March 26, 2026**.
*   Duration: **8 Hours**.

**⚙️ Build Phase**
*   Shortlisted teams will work on developing the idea submitted in Phase 1.
*   Teams are expected to build a **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** or demonstrable solution.

**⬆️ Submission Requirement**
*   Teams must upload their work to a **GitHub repository**.
*   The repository must include:
    *   **Source code / project files**.
    *   A **README** with an explanation and **setup instructions**.
*   The repository link must be submitted by **4:00 PM**.

**👨‍⚖️ Evaluation Process**
*   Evaluation will be conducted through **jury-to-team interactions**.
*   Judges will visit each team for a **one-on-one discussion and demonstration**.
*   Jury interaction begins at **3:00 PM**.

---

## ⚖️ Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on:

*   **Innovation & Originality:** Uniqueness and creativity of the idea.
*   **Technical Implementation:** Functionality, complexity, and execution.
*   **Practical Relevance / Impact:** Real-world applicability and usefulness.
*   **Clarity of Demonstration:** Ability to explain the solution effectively.
*   **Documentation Quality:** Completeness and clarity of the **Git repository and README**.

---

## 📌 General Guidelines

*   Teams must adhere to **ethical practices and originality**.
*   Use of **open-source resources is permitted**; however, **proper attribution is expected**.
*   Any form of **plagiarism may lead to disqualification**.
*   Teams must **bring their own laptops and required resources**.
